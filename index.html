Mobile Application for Delivering Drinking Water Cans

Access to clean water is a critical issue in many parts of India, with 40% of the population lacking freshwater as per the United Nations. In Bagalkot, the Krishna and Ghataprabha rivers are heavily polluted, forcing people to rely on fluoride-contaminated borewell water. While water purification plants are being established, there is no effective platform for delivering clean water. This project aims to address this problem by providing cost-effective, timely, and hygienic doorstep delivery of pure drinking water, helping people overcome challenges like transportation, time constraints, and pollution.

IOT Project to Check the Purity Of Food

Adulteration degrades food quality by adding harmful substances, making it challenging for consumers to detect. Current detection methods are complex, expensive, and often require laboratory settings. This paper proposes a cost-effective, portable IoT sensor for real-time detection of adulterants, such as papaya seeds in pepper. In India, nearly 25% of food samples analyzed in 2017-18 were found to be adulterated, highlighting the urgency of effective detection. Milk, a common target for adulteration due to its nutrient value, faces significant challenges with both qualitative and quantitative detection methods. The motivation for food fraud often stems from economic factors, posing serious public health risks, especially in developing regions.
